Finding 01 — #1 ranked

Memory and continuity is the dominant unmet need

Users don't want more features. They want the AI to remember them — decisions made, constraints set, prior attempts, project context. Every session starting from zero is the top complaint across every platform.

23 distinct sources 5,000+ engaged users All 3 primary corpora
"Every session, I end up re-explaining: why decisions were made, previous failed attempts, project-specific constraints, debugging history, feature intent." — r/ObsidianMD
Finding 02

Trust is the gating constraint — not features, not price

16 distinct sources. 855+ minimum engaged users. Users will give an AI their life context — if they can see it, edit it, and know it won't leak. This is not a privacy policy problem. It's a product design problem.

16 distinct sources 855+ engaged users All 3 primary corpora
"I don't know what it knows about me. I don't know where it uses it. I don't know how to delete it." — r/ChatGPT (composite from multiple threads)
Finding 03

Hallucination — especially false confidence about user-provided context

The life-assistant-specific failure mode: "pretends it read the file." Not just wrong answers — confidently wrong answers about things the user explicitly provided. Confirmed across all 3 primary sources.

All 3 primary corpora
Finding 04

Instruction-following degrades within a session

#2 ranked complaint in the ChatGPT research run. Users set constraints at the start of a session; the AI ignores them by the end. Directly undermines any relationship-building behavior.

All 3 primary corpora
Finding 05

Agentic products that don't actually act

Motion, Reclaim, Copilot — all named. Users bought the "AI that does things for you" promise. What they got was a chatbot with a calendar integration. The gap between the pitch and the product is the complaint.

All 3 primary corpora

The divergence that defines the bet

The research splits on how to frame the memory problem. The framing determines the product response.

What everyone else is solving

Context window is too small. Tokens get cut. Advertised limits aren't real. Fix: more tokens.

What users are actually describing

Continuity of decisions, constraints, prior attempts, and project state. A relationship problem, not an architecture problem.

Bottom line

The market has told us what it wants. Clearly, repeatedly, at scale.

An AI that remembers you — not just facts, but context, decisions, constraints, history. An AI you can trust with that information because you can see it, edit it, and know it won't be used against you.

The research didn't generate this insight. It confirmed the problem we've been building toward for 6 months. The question is whether we build fast enough to own it.
