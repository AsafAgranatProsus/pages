What users actually do when AI assistants fail them
Behavioral signal from 8 primary research corpora. Not what users say they want — what they built, abandoned, disabled, and complained about loudly enough to count.
Signal 01 · Memory
DANGIT
a browser extension users built to fix memory themselves
Users coded their own memory
A developer built and shipped a browser extension whose sole function is to re-inject context summaries into ChatGPT every time it forgets. They named it after the expletive users say when the assistant loses thread. The tool exists because the native memory system fails consistently enough to justify engineering around it.
ChatGPT keeps forgetting what we decided earlier in long conversations… Three hours into a session and the AI completely ignores constraints we set at the start.
Signal 02 · The Jarvis Experiment
1 of dozens
features kept after one month of real use
One feature survived
One user spent a month building a comprehensive personal AI — scheduling, email, research, fitness, daily planning. After 30 days of real use, they kept exactly one integration: the gym workout generator. Every other connection was abandoned. The surviving feature had three properties: recurring need, clear timing, genuine complexity. The rest didn't.
I built a personal AI Jarvis connected to my entire life. After a month, I only use it for gym workouts… The places where personal AI actually sticks are narrow and specific: recurring need + clear timing + genuine complexity.
Signal 03 · Daily Planning
1,836
Motion reviews analyzed by NLP — 33.4% negative sentiment
1,836 calendars fought back
JustUseApp's NLP analysis of Motion reviews finds users reporting ~20% of auto-scheduled blocks require manual rework — the calendar gets reorganized by hand to undo the AI. The product that promised to optimize time created more overhead than it removed. Users didn't ask for a smarter calendar. They asked to see their day clearly. The AI gave them a scheduling engine instead.
The AI just dumps tasks into available timeslots without optimizing my schedule, often leaving large blocks of time wasted. I end up manually re-organizing everything which defeats the purpose of Motion.
Signal 04 · Proactivity — Failure
Stop
the first word in the Reddit thread title
Don't text me about my wife
A ChatGPT user received an unsolicited watch notification offering to help plan a meal with their spouse. They went to Reddit to ask how to make it stop — framing the notification as a violation, not a feature. The assistant assumed intimacy it hadn't earned. That's the failure mode: initiating the relationship, not just the task.
Got a notification from ChatGPT on my watch… it said next time I'm in with my wife it can help pick a film and plan a meal. Totally unprompted… Do they actively want to drive people away, or what?
Signal 05 · Proactivity — Success
1
notification, user-authorized domain, kept in the stack
Call your mom
A user configured a WhatsApp-connected AI to watch for one specific value — family maintenance — and got pinged at the right moment to call their mother. They posted about it positively and kept the setup. The shape of proactivity that works: user-authorized domain, narrow trigger, single notification, no commentary. The exact inverse of Signal 04.
An AI assistant I set up on WhatsApp pinged me [to call my mom]. It's a small thing… needed software to notice.
Signal 06 · Relationship Layer
27 upvotes · 30 comments
r/ChatGPT thread on synthetic empathy — attitudinal signal
Stop pretending to care
A Reddit post critiquing AI assistants' "coddling and gaslighting" tone drew 27 upvotes and 30 comments of agreement. Users described simulated empathy as "creepy and disgusting" — explicitly preferring input-output operations over performed human connection. Note: this is attitudinal, not behavioral. The synthesis does not record users abandoning products specifically over warmth. The signal is real; the behavioral weight is lower than Signals 01–05.
I hate how that software is trying to constantly coddle and gaslight me into anthropomorphising it. It's a program built on a binary system. I want it to fulfil input-output operations… I definitely don't want it to mimic humans. That's just incredibly creepy and disgusting.
The Open Question · What this means for Toqi
The Jarvis experiment is the answer to the Fork
Signal 02 resolves the strategic question that the synthesis left open: users try the comprehensive vision, then fall back to one bounded job. The dinner bet fits the surviving-feature shape — recurring need, clear timing, genuine complexity. Sprawling daily-planning ambition does not. The question is whether Toqi builds toward the comprehensive vision (and accepts the abandonment rate) or bets on being the one feature that survives.
Path A · Stronger in data
Nail one job, earn the right to expand
Start with the dinner bet — it fits the surviving-feature shape. Build trust through reliability on a narrow, recurring need. Expand only when the user signals readiness. The Jarvis experiment says this is the only path that doesn't end in abandonment.
Path B · Requires product signal
Lead with the relationship, let jobs follow
Toqi's bet is the relationship layer — memory, continuity, trust. If the relationship is strong enough, users may tolerate a broader surface. Signal 06 (warmth backlash) is the risk: relationship must be earned through continuity, not performed through warmth. No behavioral data yet confirms this path survives the Jarvis test.
