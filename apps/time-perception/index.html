Rooms do not forget. They absorb the weight of every hour spent inside them — the particular quality of light at 4pm in November, the sound a chair makes when someone who is no longer here used to sit in it. The wallpaper has been watching. It has been watching since before you arrived. It will continue watching after you leave. This is not a metaphor. The pattern repeats but it is never quite the same twice. You have been counting the repetitions without knowing it. You have lost count. You started over. You lost count again.
